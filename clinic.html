We’re Invested in Your Recovery

At Focus Physiotherapy, we take a unique approach to rehabilitation healthcare services, drawing from a wide range of restorative techniques to help you achieve a return to healthy, active living following injury. If you’re an athlete, we can also introduce you to strategies to improve strength, technique, and performance.

The Focus Physiotherapy Process

When you’re hiking in the mountains one day and can’t get up the stairs the next, it’s a sharp reminder that quality of life is more than a phrase—it’s a precious commodity. Your recovery, and return to normal activity, is our priority. As Registered Physiotherapists, we’re here to help you reach your goals and maximize your quality of life. We’ll approach your rehabilitation in four steps:
1. A Complete and Thorough Examination—taking into account all referral information, medical history, lifestyle habits, along with your personal goals and observations. Our examination will determine where pain and sensitivity exist and if there are any biomechanical problems.
2. A Confirmed Diagnosis—not just attributing a name to your condition, but also identifying other areas of your body that might be affected by your injury. We also teach you about your condition and its causes, so that you are able to make informed changes and avoid recurrences.
3. A Personalized Treatment Plan—which will determine the best course of treatments to begin your healing—adjusted as necessary with progress. Your treatment plan will likely involve a combination of therapists: Registered Physiotherapists, Registered Massage Therapists, Kinesiologists, and Assistant Therapists, depending on the nature of your injury and its response to treatment. If necessary, we’ll also refer you to one of our Specialist Healthcare Partners for further opinion or investigation, like X-rays, for example.
4. Home Exercises—are important, because we want you to take an active role in your treatment. It’s very likely that we’ll assign you some strengthening exercises to complete when at home. Done right, these will help speed up your recovery and prevent re-injury.
